## Discussion (Free-Choice)
For the free-choice analytics system, I explored Plausible Analytics as an alternative to Google Analytics and LogRocket. I wanted something that would represent a different form of analytics. Plausible is privacy-focused and the behavior visualizations are also really neat. It emphasizes privacy, simplicity, and minimal data collection while also allowing you to get to know your customer base (compared to invasive tracking in Google Analytics). Plausible is also open-source program that does not use cookies and does not store personal data.


Other reasons why I chose Plausible Analytics was because of the ease of integration (like GA and LogRocket). All I had to do was paste scripts tag into every html page that I wanted tracked. Once done, it began the logging immediately. I also tracked total visitors, top pages, referral sources, device types, and geographic regions of where my page visitors were from.
